My Projects

Interaction Design Individual Project: SmartPark

Summary

This is the proejct I worked on for my interaction design course. I designed an integrated parking information system that allows users to keep track of different available parking options at downtown Ann Arbor and at the University of Michigan campus.

1. First part of my design process focused on problem framing.

To frame my problem, I first conducted background research on campus parking and found that parking at university is a real problem that people complain about. Interestingly, my needfinding interviews indicated opposite results that it is not difficult to find parking at the University of Michigan campus and off-campus within acceptable walking distance. However, all of the interviewees complained about the lack of clear parking infomration, including street parking, garage parking and permit parking information. Thus, I changed my design problem from helping people to find parking spot into improving people's experiences finding parking spot.

2. Then I explored the solution space by brainstorming and sketching out the solutions.

3. The next process dealt with finding the one good solution.

In this process, I develop three personas to represent the needs of three primary users, students, faculties and visitors.

Based on their needs, I followed the Questions, Options and Criteria (QOC) approach to narrow down my solution space. The one final solution I chose to develop on is an integrated parking information app because it can provide users the most comprehensive parking information.

4. Finally, prototypes!

In this final stage of design process, user storymap and user flow were constructed to build the foundation for later low and high fidelity protytyping.
